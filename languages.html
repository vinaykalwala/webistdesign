According to the National Language Of India, There are a maximum of 22 languages are used officially, in India. Actually, there are a lot of languages are being used across India, but most of them are verbal only, which makes them not eligible to get certified as authorized languages.
Most of the Indian languages do not have proper writing, so they became useful only for local communication rather than making the language widely popular. According to a lot of surveys, there have been more than one thousand languages are talked in and used for communication.
But as time passed by only a few of them, which have a proper-native-speakers, and good verbal and written language remained in use. Although a lot of languages do have a beautiful hearing accent and meaning, unfortunately, due to lack of writing availability, they became extinct.
 Some of the widely used and National languages of India’s recognized languages are listed below. Assamese, Bengali, Bodo, Dogri, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Kashmiri, Konkani, Maithili, Malayalam, Manipuri, Marathi, Nepali, Odia, Punjabi, Sanskrit, Santali, Sindhi, Tamil, Telugu, and Urdu are only a few of the 22 languages spoken.
 Hindi, for instance, is a widespread language, and definitely one of the most famous and used languages all over India. The Indian education system teaches every student clearly that ‘if you know how to communicate in Hindi, then you, probably, know how to survive in any part of India.’ Thus, one can know the significance of the Hindi language and that is why it is rated as a highly useful language among others.
A whopping 43.6 percent of the people over 1.4 billion citizens of India talk in Hindi, which makes a whole new point to be thought about it. There are 52.83 crore speakers interacting in this ancient language.
